Power Analysis Visualisation

These pages provide tools to determine the sample size or supervision requirements of an experiment, given details of that experiment.

Sample size determination in a fully supervised experiment (A Priori Power Analysis)

For a fully labelled experiment, i.e. where all examples are labelled either positive or negative, this page allows the use of power analysis to determine the sample size required. The sample size will depend on the acceptable risk of a false positive, the acceptable risk of a false negative, and the size of the effect being tested for.

Sample size determination in a PU experiment (A Priori Power Analysis)

The page is concerned with Positive/Unlabelled (PU) data. That is, data where some examples have positive labels, and some examples have no labels and could be positive or negative. As in the page above, this page allows for the determination of a sample size required to observe a given effect size, given the risk of a false positive and the risk of a false negative. Two other variables also affect the sample size required, the proportion of labelled sampled, and the prior knowledge of the number of positive examples in the population.

Supervision determination in a PU experiment

This page allows the novel capability of supervision determination. As in the previous page, this is looking at an experiment to detect a given effect size, with a given chance of a false positive, and a given chance of a false negative. However in this case, the number of samples is fixed, and the number of labelled samples required is determined.

These pages were created by Nathan Messer as part of a MSc thesis at the Department of Computer Science at the University of Manchester, to demonstrate, visualise and allow the use of the work done by Konstantinos Sechidis, Borja Calvo and Gavin Brown on Statistical Hypothesis Testing in Positive Unlabeled Data.

The paper:

Statistical Hypothesis Testing in Positive Unlabeled Data.
Konstantinos Sechidis, Borja Calvo and Gavin Brown.
